I've shipped 5 apps with Expo. The math is simple:

**Time saved per app per year:**
- No Xcode/Gradle debugging: ~20 hours
- OTA updates instead of full releases for JS-only changes: ~10 hours
- EAS Build instead of local builds: ~15 hours
- Config plugins instead of manual native setup: ~10 hours

That's roughly 55 hours per app per year. Across 5 apps, that's 275 hours, or about 7 work weeks.

The tradeoff: occasionally I hit a native module that Expo doesn't support yet. When that happens, I write a config plugin or use a development build. It's happened maybe 3 times in 4 years.

For teams with dedicated iOS/Android engineers, bare React Native makes more sense. For solo developers and small teams, Expo is the obvious choice.
